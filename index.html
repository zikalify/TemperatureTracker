How Symphony Works

When does a new cycle start?

A new cycle begins on your first day of full bleeding (Light, Medium, or Heavy). Spotting alone does not start a new cycle.

What counts as fertile mucus?

Both Clear/Slippery and Cloudy/Damp mucus are considered fertile signs. Clear/Slippery indicates peak fertility, while Cloudy/Damp indicates potential fertility.

Why am I still "Fertile" after my mucus dries up?

Symphony applies a 3-Day Rule. Your fertile window stays open for 3 full days after your last observation of fertile mucus, providing a safety margin.

How is ovulation confirmed?

Symphony looks for a temperature shift: 3 consecutive days of temperatures that are at least 0.2°C higher than the highest of the previous 6 days. Once confirmed, you enter the Low Fertility (Luteal) phase.

What if I don't track my temperature (BBT)?

If no temperature is recorded, the app relies on your mucus observations. It will assume you are in the post-ovulatory (Low Fertility) phase 3 days after your last Clear/Slippery observation.
